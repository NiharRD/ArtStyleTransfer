### Object Identity

**Object Name:**  
Taj Mahal

**Historical, Religious, or Cultural Significance:**  
The Taj Mahal is an iconic mausoleum located in Agra, India. Built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, it stands as a symbol of eternal love. It's also a UNESCO World Heritage Site, renowned for its architectural beauty and cultural significance.

### Visual Construction

**Form:**  
The structure features a large, central dome flanked by four minarets and smaller domes. The building exhibits symmetry and harmony, reflective of classic Mughal architecture.

**Material, Texture, and Style:**  
The Taj Mahal is known for its white marble construction, though this image stylizes it digitally, emphasizing bold outlines and simplified details.

### Color & Light

**Colors Used:**  
The image uses a range of colors to simulate a sunset effect, with warm yellows and oranges dominating, reflecting on the building and pool.

**Light and Shadow:**  
The use of warm light suggests a sunset, casting elongated shadows that enhance its majestic and serene presence.

### Symbolic Meaning

**Concept or Narrative:**  
The Taj Mahal symbolizes love and loss, as well as the architectural grandeur of the Mughal era.

**Universally Symbolic or Culturally Specific:**  
While culturally rooted in Indian history, its symbolism of love and beauty is universally recognized.

### Functional Role in Composition

**Central Motif or Supportive Detail:**  
The Taj Mahal is the central motif, capturing focus with its grandeur and symmetry.

**Emotional or Spiritual Response:**  
It aims to evoke feelings of awe, reverence, and contemplation, highlighting its romantic and historic themes.
