### Object 1: The Figure

**Object Identity**

- **What is the object called?**
  - The figure is often referred to as "The Screamer" or "The Figure."

- **What is its historical, religious, or cultural significance?**
  - The figure symbolizes existential anxiety and human despair. It captures universal human emotions and is a pivotal symbol of modern existential angst.

**Visual Construction**

- **Describe form (shape, posture, position).**
  - The figure is distorted, with a fluid, exaggerated shape, clutching its face with hands, mouth open wide in a scream, and eyes wide.

- **Describe material, texture, and style (e.g., sculptural, painted, digital, abstract, naturalistic).**
  - Painted in a fluid, abstract style with bold, swirling brushstrokes typical of Expressionism.

**Color & Light**

- **What color(s) are used and why?**
  - The figure is pale with shades of orange, contrasting with darker clothing, creating a stark expression of terror.

- **How does light or shadow reinforce the object's meaning?**
  - The contrast and shadow around the figure heighten its sense of isolation and panic.

**Symbolic Meaning**

- **What concept or narrative does the object express?**
  - Represents the anxiety of the human condition and deep existential terror.

- **Is it universally symbolic or culturally specific?**
  - It is universally symbolic, resonating across cultures as a depiction of fear and anxiety.

**Functional Role in Composition**

- **Is it a central motif or supportive detail?**
  - It is the central motif, capturing immediate attention and evoking emotional responses.

- **What emotional or spiritual response does it aim to provoke?**
  - It aims to provoke feelings of empathy, discomfort, and reflection on personal existential fears.
