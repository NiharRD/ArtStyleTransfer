1. Claude Monet, Impression: Sunrise, 1872. Oil on canvas, 1 in. 7 1–2 in. x 2 in. 1 1–2 in. Musée Marmottan, Paris. (Page 366)
2. A hostile critic disparagingly labeled Monet’s style “Impressionism” because of this painting’s sketchy quality and prominent brush strokes. (Page 366)
3. Monet and his circle, however, embraced the label for their movement. (Page 366)
4. A hostile critic applied the label “Impressionism” in response to the painting Impression: Sunrise exhibited in the first Impressionist show in 1874. (Page 365)
5. Although the critic intended the label to be derogatory, by the third Impressionist show in 1878, the artists had embraced it and were calling themselves Impressionists. (Page 365)
6. In sharp contrast to traditional artists, Monet painted outdoors, which sharpened his focus on the roles light and color play in capturing an instantaneous representation of atmosphere and climate. (Page 365)
7. Scientific studies of light and the invention of chemically synthesized pigments increased artists’ sensitivity to the multiplicity of colors in nature and gave them new colors for their work. (Page 365)
